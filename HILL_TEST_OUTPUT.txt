============================================================
READING
------------------------------------------------------------
Under the bilinear toxic term the washout state E0 = (10, 0) is a stable
sink (transverse eigenvalue -0.1545), and two of the five sampled starts
(low biomass, high pollutant) collapse to it: the system is bistable.

Under the saturating (Hill) toxic term the per-capita toxic mortality at
P0 = 10 drops from gamma*P0 = 0.5 to gamma*P0/(K_T+P0) = 0.045, so the
transverse eigenvalue at E0 turns positive (+0.30): the washout state
loses stability, ALL five starts recover, and the recovery basin grows
from 0.891 to 0.971. The bistability of the bilinear model is therefore
contingent on the toxic response not saturating; the bilinear form is a
conservative, worst-case description of remediation failure.
